# ThriftyChef Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 16 July 2026  

Academic prototype developed as part of the MSc Artificial Intelligence programme at Dublin Business School.

ThriftyChef (“the App”) helps users reduce food waste by tracking fridge ingredients and recommending recipes. This Privacy Policy explains what information the App collects, how it is used, and your choices.

## 1. Who we are

ThriftyChef is a student/academic prototype. For privacy questions related to this App, contact the developer via the project repository: [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/NJDBSProjects20093736/FridgeWise/issues).

## 2. Information we collect

Depending on how you use the App, we may process:

- **Profile preferences** — dietary requirements, allergies, nutrition preferences, cuisine preferences, and related settings.
- **Fridge and shopping data** — ingredients you add, quantities, storage location, expiry information, shopping-list items, and cooking progress.
- **Usage data within the App** — recipe views, ratings, meal plans, leftovers requests, and similar in-app actions needed to provide recommendations.
- **Device permissions** — camera access only if you choose to scan product barcodes. Camera frames are used for barcode detection and are not stored as photo libraries by the App.
- **Technical data** — basic app/local preferences (for example theme mode) stored on your device.

The current demo experience may use a shared demo user profile for evaluation. Do not enter sensitive personal information you do not want associated with a prototype system.

## 3. How we use information

- to generate personalised recipe recommendations and explanations;
- to apply safety filters such as allergies and dietary preferences;
- to prioritise ingredients approaching expiry;
- to support barcode product lookup, meal planning, leftovers ideas, and shopping lists;
- to improve reliability of this academic prototype.

We do not sell your personal information.

## 4. Where information is stored

- **On your device** — some preferences and local caches may be stored using on-device storage.
- **On backend services** — if the App is connected to the ThriftyChef API / database configured for your deployment, profile and fridge-related data may be sent to that backend to power recommendations.

## 5. Third-party content and services

The App may show or access third-party content such as recipe catalogue data, product/barcode metadata, nutrition or shelf-life reference data, and image URLs used for recipe presentation. Those sources are used to provide App features. Their own terms and privacy practices may also apply when content is retrieved from external services.

## 6. Data retention

Information is retained only as long as needed to operate the prototype, support academic evaluation, or as required by the hosting configuration used for your deployment. You may clear local app data by uninstalling the App or clearing app storage on your device.

## 7. Your choices

- You can edit profile preferences in the App at any time.
- You can add, edit, or remove fridge and shopping-list items.
- You can deny camera permission; barcode entry can still be done manually where available.
- You can contact us via GitHub Issues to request help with prototype data questions.

## 8. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your region).

## 9. International / academic use

This App is provided as an educational prototype. Hosting locations depend on the deployment environment used for testing or demonstration.

## 10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as the prototype evolves. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when revisions are published.
